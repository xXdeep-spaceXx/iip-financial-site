Why It Matters

Protection That Outlasts You

Life insurance is one of the most direct ways to ensure that the people who depend on you — your spouse, your children, your business partner — are protected if something unexpected happens.

But life insurance isn't just about death benefits. Modern policies can also serve as tax-advantaged savings vehicles, supplement retirement income, and protect against the financial devastation of chronic illness or long-term care needs.

At IIP, we help you identify the right type and amount of coverage — and we make sure it fits cleanly into your broader retirement and financial plan.

When You Need Coverage
👨‍👩‍👧

Income Replacement

If your family depends on your income, life insurance replaces that income — paying for housing, education, and daily life expenses if you're no longer there.

🏠

Mortgage & Debt Protection

Ensure your surviving spouse isn't left with a mortgage or outstanding debts they can't afford on a single income.

🌿

Legacy & Estate Planning

Life insurance proceeds pass to beneficiaries income-tax-free, making it one of the most efficient legacy transfer tools available.

💼

Business Continuity

Buy-sell agreements funded by life insurance ensure a business survives the death of a partner without financial chaos.

Coverage Options

Types of Life Insurance We Offer

Each type serves a different purpose. We'll help you choose the right one — or a combination — based on your goals.

Term Life Insurance

The simplest and most affordable form of coverage. You pay a fixed premium for a set term (10, 20, or 30 years). If you die during that term, your beneficiary receives the death benefit. If you outlive the policy, coverage ends.

  • Lowest cost per dollar of coverage
  • Best for income replacement during working years
  • Ideal for covering a mortgage or raising children
  • Can often be converted to permanent coverage
Most Affordable

Whole Life Insurance

Permanent coverage that never expires and builds guaranteed cash value over time. Premiums are fixed for life, and the policy accumulates a savings component you can borrow against or surrender.

  • Lifetime coverage — no expiration
  • Guaranteed cash value growth
  • Death benefit is income-tax-free to beneficiaries
  • Premiums are level — no surprises
Lifetime Coverage

Indexed Universal Life (IUL)

A flexible permanent policy that links cash value growth to a market index — similar to fixed index annuities. Growth potential with downside protection. Premiums can be adjusted over time.

  • Flexible premium payments
  • Market-linked cash value growth with floor protection
  • Tax-advantaged access to cash value in retirement
  • Death benefit can be adjusted as needs change
Growth + Protection

Final Expense Insurance

A smaller whole life policy designed specifically to cover funeral costs, medical bills, and other end-of-life expenses — so your family doesn't have to. Simple underwriting, often no medical exam required.

  • Coverage amounts typically $5,000 – $50,000
  • Simplified issue — often no medical exam
  • Premiums never increase
  • Cash value builds over time
Easy Approval
Coverage Amount

How Much Coverage Do You Need?

There's no universal answer — but these are the key factors we look at when sizing your policy.

01

Income Replacement

A common starting point is 10–12x your annual income. This gives your family time to adjust, pay off debts, and stabilize financially without rushing decisions.

02

Outstanding Debts

Add up your mortgage balance, car loans, student loans, and credit cards. Your policy should cover these so your survivors aren't burdened with payments they can't make alone.

03

Future Obligations

Factor in college tuition, childcare costs, and any other future expenses that depend on your income — costs that don't disappear when you do.

04

Existing Assets

Subtract savings, existing insurance, and other liquid assets. Life insurance fills the gap — not redundantly replaces what's already covered.

05

Spouse's Income

If your spouse earns income, factor that in. You may need less coverage than a single-income household — or you may both need separate policies.

06

Legacy Goals

If leaving an inheritance or funding a charitable gift is important to you, your policy size should reflect that intention — not just minimum survivability needs.
