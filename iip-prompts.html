Prompt C — Video Transition Video Model

Paste into Kling, Runway Gen-4, Pika, or Higgsfield · Upload Prompt A as start frame, Prompt B as end frame

START FRAME: A fully assembled glowing 3D atom on pure white background — deep plum purple nucleus (#7e3f98), three translucent glass orbital rings at 0°, 60°, and 120°, three violet electron nodes glowing softly on each ring. The atom is centered, still, architectural.

END FRAME: The same atom fully deconstructed — all components floating in space on white background: nucleus, three rings, three electron nodes, and inner glow core, each separated and hovering with precise spacing along a vertical explosion axis.

TRANSITION: The atom begins assembled and completely still. After a 0.5 second pause, the components begin to separate with deliberate, mechanical precision — as if being pulled apart by invisible forces. The three orbital rings lift away from the nucleus first, each translating outward along its own axis of rotation. Then the electron nodes detach and float to their positions beside their rings. Finally the nucleus separates into its outer shell and inner glow core. Each piece moves with eased motion (slow-in, slow-out) and a slight rotation to reveal its 3D form. The separation cascades over 2.5 seconds — not all at once. Final floating arrangement holds for 1 second.

STYLE: Photorealistic 3D render, pure white background throughout, consistent studio lighting. No camera movement — locked-off tripod shot. The only motion is the deconstruction. Satisfying, ASMR-like precision. Think Apple product reveal meets scientific visualization.

DURATION: 5–6 seconds total.
ASPECT RATIO: 16:9
QUALITY: High fidelity, smooth 24fps or higher, no artifacts.
